"The Emperor and the Kite"

"El Emperador y la Cometa"




11. She was not even allowed to bring a grain of rice to the meal, so little was she that in fact she was insignificant.



12. The emperor often forgot he had a fourth daughter at all. And so, Djeow Seow ate by herself. And she talked to herself.



13. And she played by herself, which was the loneliest thing of all. Her favorite toy was a kite of paper and sticks.



14. Every morning, when the wind came from the east past the rising sun, she flew her kite.



15. And every evening, when the wind went to the west past the setting sun, she flew her kite.



16. Her toy was like a flower in the sky. And it was like a prayer in the wind.



17. In fact a monk who passed the palace daily made up a poem about her kite.



18. My kite sails upward. Mounting to the high heavens. My soul goes on wings.



19. But then, he was a monk, and given to such thoughts..



20. As for Princess Djeow Seow, she thanked him each day for his prayer.
